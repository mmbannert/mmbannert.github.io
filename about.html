I'm a cognitive neuroscientist working as a postdoc at the Werner Reichardt Centre for Integrative Neuroscience and the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems in TÃ¼bingen. The aim of my research is to understand how our brains enable us to perceive and make correct inferences about our surroundings given the very limited information that is available to our senses. I use modern neuroimaging techniques and psychophysical modeling to understand the complex relationships between brain, mind, and the outside world. To address my research questions, I have been using color vision as a model system as well as motion processing. More recently, I became became interested in computer vision. The fascinating progress in artificial intelligence raises the question how comparisons between biological and artificial cognitive systems (i.e., brains and machines) can enhance our understanding of the human mind and behavior.
